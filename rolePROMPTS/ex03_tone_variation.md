# Exercise 3: Tone Variation with the Same Task

---

## Objective
Control tone and style with explicit instructions while keeping the core content consistent.

---

## Task Used

Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview.

---

## Variation 1: Friendly Tone

### Prompt

> "Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview using a warm and welcoming tone and show enthusiasm"

### Model Output

**Subject:** We'd Love to Meet You!

Hi [Candidate Name],

I hope you're doing well! We were really impressed with your application and would be excited to invite you to interview for the position at our company.

We'd love the opportunity to learn more about your experience and share more about the role and our team. Please let us know your availability this week so we can schedule a time that works best for you.

Looking forward to speaking with you soon!

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## Variation 2: Professional Tone

### Prompt

> "Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview using a formal and professional tone and avoid overly casual language. Keep it structured and concise"

### Model Output

**Subject:** Interview Invitation

Dear [Candidate Name],

Thank you for your application for the position at our organization. After reviewing your qualifications, we would like to invite you to attend an interview to further discuss your experience and suitability for the role.

Please reply with your availability over the next few days so we may schedule the interview at a mutually convenient time.

We look forward to your response.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
[Your Title]

---

## Comparison and Analysis

### Friendly Tone Analysis:
The friendly version uses warm expressions such as "I hope you're doing well" and "We were really impressed," which make it conversational and welcoming. Words like "excited" and "we'd love" show enthusiasm and make the tone more personal. The casual greeting "Hi" and sign-off "Best regards" reinforce the approachable nature.

### Professional Tone Analysis:
The professional version removes casual phrases and uses formal expressions such as "After reviewing your qualifications" and "We would like to invite you." The structure is more direct and concise. The formal greeting "Dear" and sign-off "Sincerely" with title inclusion establish professional distance.

### Key Observation:
The core message remains identical in both versions — inviting the candidate to an interview and requesting their availability. However, the tone instructions dramatically changed the delivery style. The friendly version prioritizes warmth and connection, while the professional version prioritizes clarity and formality.

**This demonstrates that tone control through explicit prompting allows the same content to be adapted for different audiences and contexts without changing the fundamental information being communicated.**
