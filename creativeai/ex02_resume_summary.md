# Exercise 2: Personalized Resume Summary

## Objective
Apply prompting to professional use cases by generating role-specific resume summaries and comparing how outputs change depending on the role and skills provided.

---

## Test 1: Teacher

**Prompt:**
> "Create a 4-sentence professional summary for a teacher skilled in Mathematics and Physics."

**Model Output:**

A dedicated and results-driven teacher with strong expertise in Mathematics and Physics, committed to fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills in students. Proven ability to design engaging lesson plans that simplify complex concepts and promote academic excellence. Skilled in creating inclusive learning environments that support diverse learning needs and encourage curiosity. Passionate about continuous professional development and leveraging innovative teaching methods to improve student outcomes.

---

## Test 2: Marketer

**Prompt:**
> "Create a 4-sentence professional summary for a marketer skilled in persuasion and vocabulary."

**Model Output:**

A results-oriented marketer with exceptional persuasion skills and a strong command of vocabulary, adept at crafting compelling messages that drive engagement and conversions. Proven ability to develop strategic campaigns that resonate with target audiences across multiple channels. Skilled in storytelling, brand positioning, and content optimization to enhance customer connection and loyalty. Passionate about leveraging data insights and creative communication to deliver measurable marketing impact.

---

## Comparison Analysis

The teacher's language was that of a mentor focused on building and developing the minds of learners, while the marketer's summary was oriented towards making sales and generating revenue. The specified skills played a key role in shaping the output by ensuring the responses generated were peculiar to each profession. Personalized prompts only require the profession and qualities to be changed to produce role-specific results, and the prompt is reusable because it has the same structure — making it a flexible template that can be applied across different industries.
