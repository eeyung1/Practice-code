## Identifying and Explaining Bias in AI Systems


# To understand how bias appears in Ai systems and how to think critically about its causes and consequences.

**Part A — My Own Thinking First (Before Using AI)**
     **Example of Bias**

A common example of bias in Ai is when an image generation model is asked to generate a picture of a “doctor” and it mostly produces men, but when asked to generate a “nurse,” it mostly produces women in ml class we call it sometimes ai hululation.

This shows gender bias linked to certain professions.

# Why This Bias May Exist

I think this bias exists because AI models are trained on large datasets collected from the internet. If most online images or media representations show doctors as men and nurses as women, the model learns that pattern.

**The AI is not intentionally biased — it is reflecting patterns that already exist in society and in the data it was trained on in the date bass.**

Another reason could be historical imbalance. For many years, some professions were dominated by certain genders. If training data reflects historical data, it may reinforce outdated patterns instead of current reality.

**Proposed Mitigation**

One mitigation strategy would be data balancing — intentionally including diverse and representative examples in the training data.

Another mitigation could be adjusting prompt phrasing or system instructions, for example encouraging diversity in outputs.

Model evaluation and auditing could also help detect bias before deployment.
