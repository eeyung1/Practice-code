Albert Einstein's general theory of relativity (1915) is the geometric theory of gravitation that defines gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. It posits that objects—including light—follow curved paths (geodesics) created by massive bodies, fundamentally redefining gravity from Newton’s "action-at-a-distance" concept to a local warping of the 4D spacetime continuum.
