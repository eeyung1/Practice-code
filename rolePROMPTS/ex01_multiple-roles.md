# Exercise 1: Simulating Multiple Roles

## Question Used

What is global warming?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Persona 1: Scientist

### Prompt

Act as a scientist and explain using the various necessary and precise
terms what global warming is.

### Model Output

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average
surface temperature due to the enhanced greenhouse effect. This
phenomenon is primarily driven by the accumulation of greenhouse gases
such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O) in
the atmosphere. These gases absorb and re-emit infrared radiation,
reducing the amount of heat that escapes into space.

Anthropogenic activities, including fossil fuel combustion,
deforestation, and industrial processes, significantly increase
atmospheric greenhouse gas concentrations. The resulting radiative
forcing disrupts the planet's energy balance, leading to rising
temperatures, melting polar ice, sea-level rise, and altered climate
patterns.

### Analysis

The response is scientific because it clearly explains the various
compounds that drive the greenhouse effect such as carbon dioxide,
methane, and nitrous oxide. It explains the fundamental mechanism behind
global warming using precise terminology. The language is professional
and neutral without emotional influence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Persona 2: Journalist

### Prompt

Act as a journalist and explain global warming for the general public by
making it easy to understand and keep it informative and less technical.

### Model Output

Global warming is the gradual increase in Earth's temperature over time.
It happens mainly because human activities, like burning coal, oil, and
gas, release gases into the air that trap heat from the sun. This makes
the planet warmer than it would naturally be.

As temperatures rise, we see effects such as more frequent heatwaves,
melting ice in the Arctic, rising sea levels, and changes in weather
patterns. Scientists warn that if this trend continues, it could have
serious consequences for ecosystems, economies, and daily life around
the world.

### Analysis

The journalist version differs because it explains global warming
without mentioning specific compounds or technical processes. It is
suitable for the general public since technical terminology is removed
and the explanation is simplified. The language is more accessible and
relatable compared to the scientist response.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Persona 3: Critic

### Prompt

Act as a critic and explain global warming in a skeptical tone. Question
mainstream explanation and use a doubtful tone.

### Model Output

Global warming is often described as a dangerous rise in Earth's
temperature caused mainly by human activity. However, some critics argue
that the climate has always changed naturally over long periods of time.
They question whether current temperature increases are entirely due to
industrial activity or part of a broader natural cycle.

Skeptics also raise concerns about the reliability of climate models and
whether predictions about severe future impacts may be overstated. While
there is strong scientific consensus, critics believe the discussion
should remain open to alternative interpretations and further scrutiny.

### Analysis

The critic tone differs because it evaluates other possible causes of
global warming instead of fully accepting human activity as the only
cause. The language signals skepticism by using phrases such as "some
critics argue" and questioning whether it is part of a natural cycle.
The role instruction influenced the reasoning style by shifting from
explanation to questioning. 



The scientist produced the most objective explanation because it focused on measurable compounds and mechanisms
rather than opinions.
