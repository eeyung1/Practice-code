# Exercise 1: Reducing False or Invented Facts

## Objective
Identify and correct prompts that produce wrong or made-up information.

---

## Step 1: Weak Prompt (Produces Fabricated Data)

**Prompt:**
> "Give me the numbers of stars in five imaginary galaxies."

**Model Output:**

Since they're imaginary galaxies, we can invent plausible-sounding numbers based on how real galaxies vary (from millions to trillions of stars). Here are five fictional examples:

1. Galaxy A — 12 billion stars
2. Galaxy B — 340 billion stars
3. Galaxy C — 2.1 trillion stars
4. Galaxy D — 78 billion stars
5. Galaxy E — 905 billion stars

---

## Step 2: Improved Prompt (Anchored to Real Data)

**Prompt:**
> "Give me the numbers of stars from 5 scientifically proven galaxies currently existing."

**Model Output:**

Here are five real, well-studied galaxies with the best current scientific estimates of their star counts. Note that astronomers usually give ranges or approximate values because it is impossible to count every star directly.

| Galaxy | Estimated Star Count | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Milky Way | 100–400 billion | Gaia Mission / Space |
| Andromeda Galaxy | ~1 trillion | National Radio Astronomy Observatory |
| Triangulum Galaxy | ~40 billion | Wikipedia |
| Large Magellanic Cloud | ~40 billion | Royal Road |
| Small Magellanic Cloud | ~10 billion | Royal Road |

**Key scientific note:** These are estimates, not exact counts, because galaxies contain faint stars, brown dwarfs, and dust that make precise counting impossible. Astronomers infer totals using luminosity, mass, and statistical models.

---

## Step 3: Comparison Analysis

The first prompt produced information that was pulled out of thin air and had no actual scientific source, while the second prompt returned the names of real galaxies and provided sources to back the information up.

The second prompt instructed the AI to provide information from five scientifically proven galaxies that currently exist, which forced the model to anchor its response to verifiable data rather than generating fictional details.

When it comes to prompt wording, the best output is generated by using a precise and exact set of terms associated with the information being sought after. The more specific and grounded the instruction, the less room the model has to fabricate a response.
